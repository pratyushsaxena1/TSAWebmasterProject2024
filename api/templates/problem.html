Negative Effects of Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, are major contributors to air and water pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, and climate change. Burning fossil fuels releases harmful pollutants into the atmosphere, leading to respiratory problems, acid rain, and smog. Additionally, the extraction and transportation of fossil fuels can result in habitat destruction, water contamination, and wildlife endangerment.

WASTE MANAGEMENT

Waste generates methane, a potent greenhouse gas which is more dangerous than carbon dioxide for the ozone layer.

TRANSPORTATION

Vehicles used for transportation emit CO2 due to fossil fuel combustion.

Residential Sources of Greenhouse Gases

APPLIANCES, ELECTRONICS, LIGHTING

These devices run on electricity generated from fossil fuels, such as coal or natural gas, the associated emissions contribute to the carbon footprint of households

HEATING AND COOLING

Contribute to CO2 emissions through fossil fuel combustion (e.g., oil, natural gas) and electricity consumption for heating, cooling, and air conditioning

CONSTRUCTION

Construction and renovation activities contribute to CO2 emissions through energy-intensive processes (e.g., cement production, transportation of materials).

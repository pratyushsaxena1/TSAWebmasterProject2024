Energy Efficient Refrigerators, Washing Machines, and HVAC Systems

Upgrading to energy-efficient appliances and HVAC systems is a smart investment that reduces your utility bills by consuming less electricity, saving you money and preserving natural resources.

Cost:
  • Energy-Efficient refrigerators and washing machines may cost from $500 to $2500, depending on brand and style.
  • HVAC systems can go from $2,500 to $5,000 for a small home, while more advanced systems or those for larger homes could cost $10,000 or more.
Rebate:
  • Generally, you can get 30% of the cost of the project with a maximum amount of $600.

Changing to LED Lighting

LED bulbs use significantly less energy than traditional incandescent bulbs, while also lasting much longer, which means fewer replacements and reduced waste.

Cost:
  • On average, LED bulbs can range from $2 to $20 or more per bulb. But the potential energy savings and longer lifespan of LED bulbs often make them a cost-effective choice in the long run.
Rebate:
  • The federal government provides many rebates and incentives to switch to LED lighting. There are more rebates based on specific certifications.

Smart Thermostats

Smart thermostats can help you save money on your energy bills by learning your heating and cooling preferences and automatically adjusting the temperature accordingly. They come in a variety of tiers to fit your needs and budget.

  • Basic models typically have a programmable schedule.
  • Mid-range models may have features like occupancy sensors and smart home integration.
  • High-end models can include features like learning algorithms and weather forecasting.

Cost:

$100 - $500

Rebate:

Up to $75

Energy Star Windows

Energy Star windows can help you save money on your energy bills by reducing heat gain in the summer and heat loss in the winter. They are available in a variety of styles to match your home's aesthetics.

Cost:

$200 - $800 (including installation)

Rebate:

Up to $1200 (total for qualifying home improvements)

Rebates are available for a variety of home improvements, including windows, doors, skylights, insulation, and electrical upgrades.

Install Solar Panels

Installing solar panels harnesses the sun's power for your home or business, reducing your carbon footprint and cutting energy costs while boosting property value.

Cost:
  • Solar PV systems cost about $3 per watt installed. A 7,000 watt (7 kilowatt) system therefore costs about $21,000 to install. Such a system would provide 20 to 35 kilowatt-hours of electricity per day, depending on climate, and could meet most of a household's demand.
Rebate:
  • Generally, federal tax credit and rebate can cover up to 26% of the cost of the project. $600.
